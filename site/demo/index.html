Global Context

Visualization for timeseries interest identification

Why?

In any news story, a journalist coveys the information by answering 5 basic questions Who, Where, What, When and Why. The Global Context (GC) view of “Story Analysis” demos descriptive analytics for a sample news corpus to identify (why) major topics/incidences (what), characters/organizations (who) and locations (where) for a given date range (when). GC time filtered view can provide cognitive guidance for selecting historical topics that can be beneficial to revisit from time to time.

When?

  • 24 May 2021 - 30 May 2021
  • 24/05/2021 - 30/05/2021
  • 24/05/2021 - 30/05/2021

What?

The following section provides the top 25 weighted topics identified in the specified date range. Hovering over a topic will display the number of documents the topic was highlighted per day. This descriptive information is helpful to understand whether the topic is having more / less / consistent importance in the selected date range. Clicking on the topic will lead you to the Relative Context (RC) view of the selected topic. RC view provides a quick diagnostics analytics view for discovering sub-topics of the selected topic.


Who?

The following section provides the major characters and organizations that have been referred to in the news stories in the specified date range. Hovering over a topic will display the number of documents the topic was highlighted. This descriptive information is useful for identifying the importance of the character/organization in the corpus. Clicking on the topic will lead you to the Relative Context (RC) view of the selected topic. RC view provides a quick diagnostics analytics view for discovering sub-topics of the selected topic.



Where?
