Contextual Visualization

Global Context

Visualization for timeseries interest identification

In any news story, a journalist coveys the information by answering 5 basic questions Who, Where, What, When and Why. The Global Context (GC) view of “Story Analysis” demos descriptive analytics for a sample news/article corpus to identify (why) major topics/incidences (what), characters/organizations (who) and locations (where) for a given month (when). This visualization can work to guide identifying historical important events that require editorial recall for comprehensive writing. Clicking on the topic produces a Terms Board based on all the news/articles for the topic to aid planning ideas for writing.

To build a collection of articles for this demo project, we have collected published articles from the Pharmaletter News Publishers. For this demo, we have collected 730 articles published between 2021-03-01 and 2021-05-30. Among these articles, 273 articles were published in March, 232 articles were published in April and 225 articles were published in May. Demo provides a monthly (time-series)comprehensive data visualization on the whole collection to guide on selecting a historical topic for throwback editorial reporting. On selecting a topic a Terms Board visualization is produced for intuiting creative thoughts. A list of documents scanned for producing the Terms Board is also presented. Selecting the "Local Analysis" link of an article will visualize the story words extraction in a clockwise radial bar chart.

Why?

The answer to “Why” is contributed via an editor’s point of view for achieving a task. This visualization can be useful for the following tasks:

- Identify historically important topic in the given time that can be beneficial to recall in the current time

- Identifying decaying topics that can be beneficial to review/compare against raising new topics

What?

The following section provides the top 25 weighted topics identified in the specified date range. Hovering over a topic will display the number of documents the topic was highlighted per day. This descriptive information is helpful to understand whether the topic is having more / less / consistent importance in the selected date range. Clicking on the topic will lead you to the Terms Board view of the selected topic. Terms Board view provides a quick diagnostics analytics view for discovering ideas related to the selected topic.


Who?

The following section provides the major characters and organizations that have been referred to in the news stories in the specified date range. Hovering over a topic will display the number of documents the topic was highlighted. This descriptive information is useful for identifying the importance of the character/organization in the corpus. Clicking on the topic will lead you to the Terms Board view of the selected topic. Terms Board view provides a quick diagnostics analytics view for discovering ideas related to the selected topic.
